Extreme heat doesn't just strain your body—it shrinks your focus, slows your reactions, and makes mistakes more likely. During hot weather, even simple tasks demand more mental and physical energy, leaving you more prone to overlooking details or rushing through steps. Heat also fuels frustration and fatigue, which can lead to complacency about safety protocols. Staying aware of how temperature affects your mental state is just as important as staying hydrated. Recognizing these early signs helps you protect yourself and others before small oversights turn into serious incidents.
