As the school year kicks off, your morning routine gets busier—more traffic, tighter schedules, and a lot more on your mind. This shift can easily pull your focus away from safe driving habits. Picture this: you're running late, juggling school drop-offs and work, and your thoughts are already on the meeting ahead. You glance at the road but aren't truly *present*—your mind is still packing mental bags for the day. That moment, when your attention drifts from the road to the stress of the hour, is exactly when safety risks creep in. The back-to-school rush creates perfect conditions for distracted driving, where even a split-second lapse can turn a routine commute into a dangerous situation. Recognizing this pressure point is the first step to staying safe—because awareness lets you catch yourself before fatigue, frustration, or rushing thoughts steal your focus.
