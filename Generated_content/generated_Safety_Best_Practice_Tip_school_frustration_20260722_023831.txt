- When frustration starts bubbling up, pause and take three deep breaths to reset your focus and calm your thoughts.  
- Use a quick Rate Your State (RYS) check to honestly assess how frustrated you’re feeling and whether it’s affecting your attention or decisions.  
- If you notice frustration building during a task, step away for a brief moment to recharge — even a short walk or a glass of water can break the tension cycle.  
- Redirect that frustration into problem-solving mode: ask yourself, “What’s one small action I can take right now to move forward?”  
- Share your frustration with a trusted coworker or supervisor; a simple conversation often uncovers solutions and prevents small annoyances from becoming big errors.
