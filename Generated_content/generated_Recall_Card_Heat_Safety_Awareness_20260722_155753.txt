Heat can sneak up on you faster than you think. During July’s Heat Illness Awareness Month, remember that high temperatures don’t just affect your body—they shrink your focus, slow reactions, and make small mistakes more likely. The danger isn’t just dehydration; it’s the way heat amplifies everyday human factors like tiredness, impatience, and skipping safety steps. When you’re sweating and uncomfortable, you’re more likely to glance away or rush through tasks, which can turn a routine day into an incident. Staying cool isn’t just about water—it’s about keeping your mind sharp and your decisions sound, so you stay safe and others stay safe, too.
