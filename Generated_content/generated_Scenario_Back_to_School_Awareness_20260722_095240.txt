The morning rush was already in full swing when Maya hopped into her car, coffee in hand and a packed lunch on the passenger seat. With school starting today, traffic had thickened and her usual calm drive felt anything but. As she merged onto the highway, she noticed a group of students crossing ahead, their backpacks bouncing with each step. Maya glanced at the clock—she was running late—and her mind raced with the day’s to-do list. In that split second, she didn’t see the cyclist who pulled out from the shoulder right in front of her. Only quick steering saved her from a collision, but her heart pounded as she pulled over, realizing how easily a simple change in routine had pulled her focus away from the road.
