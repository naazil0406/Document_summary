As the school year kicks off, Jamie heads out for work with a backpack full of last-minute prep and a mind buzzing with the morning rush. With three kids to get out the door and a packed lunch forgotten on the counter, Jamie grabs the car keys and speeds toward the highway, barely noticing the growing traffic. On the way, a sudden stop for a school bus ahead makes Jamie slam on the brakes, heart racing as the car behind swerves to avoid a collision.

In that moment, the stress of the morning, the tight schedule, and the distracted thoughts about work all combine. Jamie realizes too late that focusing on the task at hand—driving safely—was overshadowed by the chaos of the morning routine. This near-miss highlights how easily changing schedules and new responsibilities can pull our focus away from safety, especially during high-stress times like the back-to-school rush.
