When the thermometer climbs, your team’s focus can too—right along with the risk. Here’s how to keep everyone sharp and safe during heatwaves:

- Take a quick Rate Your State (RYS) check before starting any task—especially when it’s hot. Notice if you’re feeling sluggish, irritable, or distracted, and address it right away.
- Keep hydration breaks short but frequent. Even mild dehydration can cloud judgment and slow reactions, turning small oversights into big problems.
- Encourage teammates to speak up if they notice someone struggling with the heat. Early intervention stops risks before they escalate.
- Plan the heaviest mental or physical work for cooler parts of the day. If that isn’t possible, build in extra safety checks and allow more time to complete tasks.
- Use simple visual reminders—like a posted chart or a quick team huddle—to reinforce heat safety habits until staying alert in the heat becomes second nature.
