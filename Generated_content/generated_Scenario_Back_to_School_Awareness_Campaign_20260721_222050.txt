Imagine it's a Tuesday morning during the back-to-school rush. You’ve got a packed schedule, and the kids are scrambling to get ready. As you pull out of your driveway, you’re still mentally juggling the morning’s chaos—lunchboxes forgotten, homework not signed, and that important meeting you’re late for. You merge onto the road, but your mind is still back at home, replaying the morning’s stress instead of focusing on traffic. Suddenly, a car pulls out in front of you, and you barely have time to react. 

In this moment, the pressure of the day’s demands and lingering stress has your attention split between home and road. Recognizing these pressures early is crucial. A quick pause and a simple self-check before you start driving can help you reset and bring your full focus back to the road, where it’s needed most.
