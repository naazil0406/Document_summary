Heat stress doesn’t just affect your body—it changes how you think and react. In July, as temperatures rise, so does the risk of human factors like rushing, frustration, and reduced focus. When you’re overheating, your brain works slower, decision-making gets clouded, and small oversights become more likely. This month, focus on checking your state regularly, staying hydrated, and taking cooling breaks. Remember, heat impacts your ability to stay alert and make safe choices, so protecting yourself and your team starts with recognizing how heat changes your state before it leads to an incident.
