As the back-to-school rush kicks in, it’s easy to get pulled in a dozen directions before you even reach work. That morning scramble can cloud your focus and set the tone for a distracted day. Here’s a simple, practical way to protect yourself and others during this hectic time: 

- Before you start the engine, take a slow, deep breath and ask yourself if you’re feeling rushed or irritated — even a quick “Rate Your State” check can reset your mindset. 
- Once you’re behind the wheel, give yourself at least one extra car length of space from the car in front of you, especially in school zones or heavy traffic. 
- If you notice your thoughts drifting to the list of things waiting at home or work, gently pull your focus back to the road and your driving. 
- Use the Habit Reminder tool to make these small pauses and extra distances a regular part of your morning routine — it only takes a few seconds to build a safer habit.
