As the school year kicks off, your morning routine gets busier—more traffic, tighter schedules, and a lot more on your mind. This shift can easily pull your focus away from safe driving habits. 

Imagine this: You’re running late, juggling school drop-offs and a packed workday ahead. The pressure makes you glance at your watch instead of the road ahead. You’re about to merge into heavy traffic when you realize a car pulled out in front of you—you didn’t see it because your eyes weren’t fully on the task. 

The back-to-school rush often brings this kind of moment. The key is recognizing when the morning chaos starts to cloud your focus and taking a quick pause to reset. A simple habit check—like keeping a safe following distance and doing a quick Rate Your State (RYS) before starting the engine—can keep you safe when the school zone lights turn green.
